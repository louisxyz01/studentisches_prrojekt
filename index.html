Types of Social Media Harassment

Cyberbullying

Repeated hostile behavior aimed at causing distress to others online.

Stalking

Unwanted surveillance and persistent unwelcome attention.

Hate Speech

Discriminatory remarks targeting specific groups or individuals.

Impact of Social Media Harassment

Mental Health

Victims often experience anxiety, depression, and PTSD. Studies show 64% of harassed individuals report significant emotional distress.

Social Isolation

Many victims withdraw from online platforms, affecting their social connections and professional networking opportunities.

Academic Performance

Students facing cyberbullying show decreased academic performance and higher dropout rates.

Key Concerns in India

    Digital Literacy Gap

    Many users lack awareness about privacy settings and online safety measures, making them vulnerable to harassment.

    Legal Framework

    The IT Act and IPC sections provide legal protection, but many victims are unaware of their rights under cyber laws.

    Regional Language Harassment

    Cyberbullying in regional languages often goes unreported due to limited content moderation in local languages.

Comprehensive Solutions

Educational Initiatives

Schools and colleges implementing cybersecurity awareness programs and digital citizenship courses.

Legal Support

Access to cyber cells and legal aid for victims of online harassment. Know your rights under IT Act sections 66A, 66C, and 66E.

Community Support

Online support groups and counseling services for victims of cyberbullying and harassment.

Prevention Strategies

Strong Privacy Settings

Regularly review and update your social media privacy settings.

Document Everything

Keep records of harassment incidents including screenshots and dates.

Report and Block

Use platform tools to report abusive behavior and block harassers.
